Yeah, let's brainstorm this! Your landing page should hook folks right away—maybe open with that hand-sketched black-and-white sailboat and Narragansett Bay graphic, clean and artsy. Headline like “Sail Cargo: Connecting Farms to Restaurants, Zero Emissions, All Community.” Subheading: “Join our network of sailors hauling local goods across the Bay.” Keep it short and punchy. Visuals? Beyond the sketch, maybe a photo carousel of sailboats in action, farms with fresh produce, and restaurants serving it up—shows the vibe without being too polished. People want to see: 1. Clear “Sign Up” button front and center—sailors and farmers/restaurants both need an easy way in. 2. A quick “How It Works” section: Sailor signs up, gets matched with cargo (say, crates of veggies), sails it to a restaurant, gets paid per trip. Farmers list goods, restaurants order, you coordinate. 3. Trust stuff—testimonials like “Moved a ton of tomatoes last season!” or “Sailors were reliable, food arrived fresh.” Maybe a badge saying “Vet Checked Crew” to ease nerves. 4. Map of the Bay with dots for farms, restaurants, and sailor home ports. Interactive’s cool if budget allows, but even static works. 5. FAQ section tackling basics: “How much do sailors earn?” “What goods can we ship?” “Is it weather-dependent?” (Spoiler: yeah, but that’s the charm.) For sign-ups, sailors need: - A form asking for boat size, experience, insurance proof, and availability. Maybe a “sailing resumé” upload—logbooks or race wins. Restaurants and farmers just need basic info: address, cargo types, volume. To vet folks, check: - Boat safety (coastal cruising standard, Coast Guard compliant?). - Sailor refs—did they finish a delivery before? - Cargo handlers—restaurants with clean prep areas, farms with pest-free produce. Quick Zoom call with everyone before onboarding. Trainings? - One-day workshop at a marina: navigation, cargo tie-downs, weather reading. - Monthly meetups for tips—how to pack veggies so they don’t bruise, or chat about best routes. - Online guide: PDF or site page on “Sail Cargo 101”—handling, timing, even cool Bay shortcuts. Workshops could be fun—invite a chef to show how they use farm goods, or a sailor to demo knots. Maybe pair with local sailing clubs for credibility. Keep it grassroots but pro. Wanna focus on one part, like the sign-up flow or vetting process? Or add a specific element, like a blog for sailor stories?
